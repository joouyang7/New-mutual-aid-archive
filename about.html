intro

Mutual aid is collective coordination to meet each other's needs, usually from an awareness that the systems we have in place are not going to meet them (Dean Spade, Mutual Aid: Building Solidarity During This Crisis (And The Next), 2020).

This project archives mutual aid relationships, entry points into movement organizing, preparation and reaction to crises, systemic failures, and the limits of mutual aid. The responses will be published in the form of a living archive on a publicly-available website. No contact information will be shared.

Jo Ouyang Brown/RISD '26 is the organizer of this project. They have been working in community and student organizing spaces for the past 5 years, specifically with Asian/American, Pro-Palestine, Abolitionist, Queer/Trans, and Art organizations on Muscogee land (Atlanta), Narragansett Indian land (Providence), and Lenapehoking land (Philadelphia).
